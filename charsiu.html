Charsiu Chicken Rice

Charsiu is a Cantonese-style of barbequeing meat, and while charsiu is usually paired with pork, you can easily substituted it with chicken too!


Picture and recipe courtesy of aheadofthyme.com

Ingredients

  • Chicken (boneless chicken breast/thigh)
  • Hoisin sauce
  • Honey
  • Vegetable oil
  • Dark soy sauce
  • Garlic
  • Ground black pepper
  • Fermented red bean curd (optional but it's what makes it red)
  • Sesame seeds for garnish

Charsiu sauce

  • 1/4 cup hoisin sauce
  • 2 tbsp honey
  • 1 tbsp fermented red bean curd
  • 1 tsp ground black pepper

Instructions

  1. Pat dry chicken with paper towel before adding seasoning.
  2. Transfer chicken to a ziploc bag and add in hoisin sauce, honey, vegetable oil, dark soy sauce and garlic. Marinade in chiller for at least an hour.
  3. Preheat oven to 400F.
  4. Bake chicken for 30 minutes. Flip halfway through baking.
  5. Whisk charsiu sauce.
  6. Remove chicken from oven and generously apply the sauce over each piece. Place the chicken back into the oven for another 3 mins.
  7. Flip the chicken and repeat step 6.
  8. Turn the broiler on high and broil the chicken for another 2-3 mins to achieve a caramel brown colour and until edges of the chicken become slightly charred.
  9. Serve with a bowl of rice and sprinkle sesame seeds for garnish.
